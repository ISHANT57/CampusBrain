# Sitare Foundation — Fresh Extract (from live sitare.org, univ.sitare.org, students.sitare.org)

Supplements `sitare.md` (which was built from this project's own site clone). This file contains only **new** information found by crawling the real, live `sitare.org` and related subdomains that wasn't already captured. Facts are paraphrased/summarized from the live sites, not copied verbatim, except for short attributed taglines/quotes. Crawled via Firecrawl (map + batch scrape) on the dates covered by this extraction.

**Deliberately excluded:** the Foundation's internal roster pages (`growth_camp_2022.php`, `hope_camp_2022.php`, `cep_9to10.php`, `growth_8to9.php`, `growth_9to10.php`) list real minors' names, student IDs, and partially-masked phone numbers as selection/admission notices. That's personal data about children, not general Foundation information, so it is not reproduced here — only the *existence and purpose* of these programs is described below.

## 1. Sitare Foundation — parent organization (new detail)

- **10th anniversary:** as of May 20, 2026, Sitare Foundation turned ten years old (a "Founders' Letter" PDF was published for the occasion, linked from the homepage).
- **Mission tagline (verbatim, short):** "Transform fifty thousand lives through education by 2050."
- **Founders' quote (short, attributed):** "Education is the only sustainable path out of poverty." — Shilpa and Amit Singhal.
- **Implementation partner:** Sitare Foundation has partnered with **Hope and Dreams Trust** (hopedreams.org), an Indian non-profit, to implement its educational programs on the ground in India.
- **How the K-12 program actually works (not previously captured):** the Foundation admits underprivileged children into its program at the middle-school stage — specifically for **Class VI entry**, via an aptitude test and an interview — and then supports them with quality education for **seven years** (through to the end of secondary school). This K-12 pipeline is separate from, and feeds into, Sitare University for those who go on to pursue Computer Science there; some Foundation students also go directly to US undergraduate universities (see Section 4).
- **Cities where the Foundation's K-12 program operates:** Gurugram, Bhopal, Indore, Jaipur, Jodhpur, and **Ajmer** (Ajmer is a city not previously listed on the university-focused site; it appears in the K-12 program's grade-progression cohort data).
- **Historical scale snapshot (as of the July 2022 university launch press release):** the Foundation had over 400 children in its K-12 program at that time, and had selected about 100 new students out of 73,000 applications received that year, from entrance exams conducted in Jaipur, Jodhpur, Bhopal, and Indore.

### Grade-progression / selection programs (existence only, no individual data)
The Foundation runs periodic "Growth Camp" and "Hope Camp" selection events (documented for 2022) where newly selected children are onboarded into the program, plus a "Growth Entrance Exam" that current students take to advance between grades (e.g., 8th→9th, 9th→10th), and a "Continuing Education Program" that offers qualifying 9th graders continuation into 10th grade. These are administered per-city (Bhopal, Indore, Jaipur, Jodhpur, Ajmer) with each selected child assigned a Student Enrollment ID (SEID).

## 2. Sitare University — origin story detail (from the original July 2022 press release)

This is the Foundation's original press release announcing Sitare University, dated **Bhopal, July 15, 2022**. It contains several details that differ from or add to what's on the current university site — treat the current site as more up to date on anything that conflicts, but these are useful historical/founding facts:

- **Originally announced initial operating location: Rajiv Gandhi Proudyogiki Vishwavidyalaya (RGPV), Bhopal** — not Shri Ramswaroop Memorial University (SRMU), Lucknow, which is where the university currently operates per the live university site. This is a genuine change between the 2022 announcement and current operations, not a data error.
- **Originally announced first-batch size: 50 students** ("intake of FIFTY students") — differs from the "23" or "24" figures cited elsewhere on the current university site for the actual first batch; likely the planned intake differed from the actual enrolled count.
- **Originally announced majors (5, per the 2022 release):** AI, Systems, Human-Computer Interactions, **Blockchain Technologies**, and Computer Security. The current university site instead lists **Data Science** as the fifth major in place of Blockchain Technologies — an apparent curriculum change since the original 2022 announcement.
- **Launch was attended by:** Smt. Yashodhara Raje Scindia (Hon. Minister for Sports and Youth Welfare, Technical Education, Skill Development and Employment, Govt. of Madhya Pradesh), Dr. Mohan Yadav (at the time, Hon. Minister for Higher Education, Govt. of Madhya Pradesh — the current site instead titles him Chief Minister, reflecting he has since been promoted/elected to that office), Sunil Kumar Gupta (Vice Chancellor, RGPV), and Amit Singhal (Founder).
- **Founder quote (short, attributed), Amit Singhal:** described the goal of producing "one thousand high quality Computer Science graduates every year in India" via free, world-class CS education for underprivileged students — consistent with the ~1,000/year-by-2030 goal already known.
- **Vishal Sikka (Founder & CEO, Vianai Systems)** gave a supporting quote on the importance of Computer Science education being broadly accessible regardless of socioeconomic status, and said he looked forward to advising the university.
- **Press/media contact for the launch (agency, not Sitare staff):** Kommune Brand Communications — Shweta Sharma / Ashwani Gupta.

## 4. US Students — Foundation graduates admitted to US universities (separate track from Sitare University)

The Foundation's `sitare.org/seniors.php` page spotlights K-12 program graduates who were admitted directly to **top US universities** for undergraduate study — this is a distinct pathway from Sitare University itself (which is an Indian B.Tech program). The original March 2022 press release describes this as "the first batch of five talented underprivileged senior secondary students who have secured admissions in renowned US universities." Five students are named on the current site (the page's own heading says "four," a minor inconsistency on their part):

- **Kusum Choudhary** — a farmer's daughter; her family farms land they don't own, keeping roughly 30% of crop earnings under their arrangement with the landowner.
- **Mahendra Kumar** — from Chohtan, Barmer District, Western Rajasthan; his father (and elder brother) work in a sheet metal factory rolling stainless steel sheets.
- **Milan Ramdhari** — his father is an auto-rickshaw driver; Milan grew up seeing his father drive more affluent children to a private school he himself couldn't attend.
- **Nisha Choudhary** — from Chawandiya village (population 2,140), Nagaur District, Rajasthan; her father is a farmer and her mother a housewife who also helps on the farm; the whole family works the land.
- **Urmila Loona** — from a small town in Jodhpur District, Western Rajasthan; her father is a security guard, her mother a housewife; she is the first in her family to attend college.

Each has an individual profile page with a short video telling their story (`sitare.org/senior_<name>.php`).
