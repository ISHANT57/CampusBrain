## About & Mission
- Program name: Sitare University Program. "World-class Computer Science education for bright students from economically disadvantaged backgrounds."
- Founded by Dr. Amit Singhal in 2022 to provide world-class Computer Science education to talented students from underprivileged backgrounds, completely free of cost.
- Mission: leaders from Silicon Valley, top American and European institutions, industry leaders, and venture capitalists came together to build a new University Program focused on Computer Science.
- Batch history: First batch (2022) had 23-24 students. Second batch (2023) had 44 students. Third batch (2024) had 158-160 students. Intent to grow to around 1,000 admissions per year by 2030. Students selected based on JEE Mains performance plus an interview.
- Current community: ~220 students across the first three years. Almost all students get a full 100% scholarship (tuition, hostel, mess fees included). Currently operating out of SRMU Lucknow.
- Nearly every student secures a paid summer internship annually, predominantly at cutting-edge startups.
- Five focus areas: Artificial Intelligence (ML, Computer Vision, NLP, Deep Learning); Systems (Cloud Computing, OS, Networks, Data Management); Human-Computer Interaction (iOS/Android Dev, HCI Design, Graphics, Web Apps); Data Science (data gathering, pipelines, processing, visualization); Computer Security (Authentication, System/Network/Code Security, Privacy and Policy).
- Curriculum overview by year: Year 1 - Python, Math, Data Structures, English Communication. Year 2 - Algorithms, Java, Systems, Databases/IR, Machine Learning. Year 3 - Frontend, Systems, more ML, Hands-on Projects, Problem Solving. Year 4 - industry (paid internships start from the first summer).
- Vision: "One of the best Computer Science educational institutes in India by 2030."
- New campus: full Private State University under the Madhya Pradesh Private University Act. Construction started on a 38-acre site near Dewas, on the Bhopal-Indore highway. Target move-in dates vary by page (Aug 2027 / Sept 2026 / July 2028) - treat as approximate.
- Currently based in Lucknow at partner university Shri Ramswaroop Memorial University (SRMU).
- Sitare Foundation was established in 2016 by Dr. Amit Singhal and Mrs. Shilpa Singhal, aiming to empower 50,000 bright underprivileged students by 2050.
- University opened in 2022 in partnership with SRMU, offering a BTech in Computer Science.

## Admissions
- Key dates (2026 cycle): Applications open 20th April 2026, deadline 31st July 2026, results 1st week of July 2026, session commences 18th August 2026, students report to Lucknow campus 17th August 2026.
- Apply online at admissions.sitare.org.
- Eligibility: primarily for academically strong students from families earning Rs. 3 lakh or less per year (fully free); families up to Rs. 9 lakh/year also welcome with reduced scholarship. Must appear for JEE Mains 2026, percentile of 85+ generally expected. Strong academic performance in Class 10 and 12 required. Admits up to 120 students each year.
- Required documents: passport photo, Aadhaar Card (front+back), parents' income certificate, Class 10 marksheet, Class 12 marksheet, JEE Mains 2026 scorecard.
- Selection process: video interview for shortlisted candidates, plus background check to verify family income.
- Only one program offered: B.Tech in Computer Science.
- Classes currently held at SRMU, Lucknow (a recognised university that awards the degree). Students may transfer to Sitare's own Dewas campus once operational, subject to UGC/AICTE regulations.
- Scholarship & Finances: total program cost Rs. 3,00,000/year, fully covered by scholarship for eligible students. Maximum any student ever pays: Rs. 2,00,000/year. Two costs NOT covered: one-time refundable caution deposit of Rs. 10,000 to SRMU, and a personal laptop (~Rs. 50,000, student must buy).
  - Base scholarship: Rs. 1,00,000/year for every admitted student.
  - CGPA-based scholarship: up to Rs. 2,00,000/year additional.
  - Income-based scholarship tiers: up to Rs. 3 lakh income -> 100%; Rs. 3-6 lakh -> 75%; Rs. 6-9 lakh -> 50%; above Rs. 9 lakh -> no additional scholarship.
  - CGPA-based scholarship tiers: 7.5+ CGPA -> full Rs. 2,00,000; 6.6-7.4 -> Rs. 2,00,000 x (CGPA - 6.5); 6.5 or below -> no CGPA-based scholarship.
  - To retain scholarship: CGPA >= 6.5, at least 90% attendance per course, pass all subjects each semester, follow Code of Conduct, provide updated income documentation annually.
  - Year 4 centers on a year-long paid industry internship; if secured, CGPA scholarship continues, otherwise student pays full Rs. 2,00,000.
  - For changes in family financial situation: contact university@sitare.org with updated documentation.
- Campus life included in program cost: on-campus hostel, meals, modern classrooms, computer labs, library access, Wi-Fi.
- Faculty drawn from top institutions like the IITs and senior industry roles.
- COCO (COding and COmmunication) program: signature summer program after 1st and 2nd years, helps students (including Hindi-medium/government school backgrounds) bridge communication gaps.
- Admissions contact: university@sitare.org, +91 78499 10085, univ.sitare.org.
- Taglines: "Your Journey to Silicon Valley Starts Here!" / "One choice today. A lifetime of difference." / "Admissions for 2026 are now open."
- Placement rate cited on Admissions page: 91.3%.

## Curriculum
Newly established curriculum integrating foundational CS with AI, Data Science, Web Development Frameworks, and Information Retrieval. Project-based learning, collaborative research, internships starting from year one. Curriculum PDF: https://univ.sitare.org/curriculum.pdf (subject to modification). Program "designed by global technology leaders and faculty from Cornell and Stanford Universities."

Categories and courses:
- Mathematics: Math 113 (Linear Algebra), CS 119 (Calculus), CS 103 (Mathematical Foundations of Computing), CS 109 (Probability for Computer Science).
- Communication: LE 101 (Intro to Communication and Ethics), LE 102P (Book Club and Social Emotional Intelligence), LE 103P (Communication and Book Club), LE 300 (Economics for Computer Science).
- Programming: CS106 (Programming Methodology in Python), CS 110 (Data Handling in Python), CS 108A (OOP), CS 108B (Advanced OOP), CS 142 (Web Applications Development), CS 147 (Human Computer Interaction).
- Core and Data: CS 105 (Intro to Computers), CS 161 (Data Structures and Algorithms), CS 162 (Advanced DSA), CS 145 (Database Management Systems), CS 276 (Search Engines and Information Retrieval), CS 246 (Mining Massive Datasets), Cornell CS 305 (Creative Problem Solving).
- System and Security: CS 107 (Computer Organization and Systems), CS 111 (Operating Systems Principles), CS 144 (Computer Networks).
- AI/ML: CS 221 (Artificial Intelligence), CS 229 (Machine Learning), CS 230 (Deep Learning), CS 231 (Applications of Deep Learning).
- Projects: CS 106P (Python Project Course), CoCo Summer, CS 250 (Software Engineering Project incl. Technical Writing), CS 251 (Machine Learning Project).
- Industry: Industry Research Project and Internship.
