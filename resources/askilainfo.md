How does Sitare's curriculum compare to traditional universities?
Sitare University
Sitare University's Twin

Our curriculum is designed as a focused Computer Science pathway rather than a broad, lecture-led degree.

Students build foundations in mathematics, programming, data structures, algorithms, systems, databases, communication, and ethics, while also working in areas such as AI, machine learning, data science, information retrieval, and web development. The main difference is the learning model: hands-on projects, research, collaborative work, and practical industry exposure are integrated early rather than treated as optional add-ons.

We also emphasize personalized support and communication, social-emotional, and ethical development. Our model culminates in a year-long industry co-op, so students spend three years building technical depth and a final year applying it in a professional setting. Traditional universities vary widely, but this sustained integration of industry work and a tightly focused CS curriculum is central to our approach.

Education can be completely free for students from families earning ₹3 lakh or less per year, provided they maintain the required academic performance, attendance, conduct, and documentation. For these students, the scholarship can cover the full program cost.

Every admitted student receives a guaranteed base scholarship of ₹1 lakh per year, subject to attendance and conduct rules. An additional scholarship of up to ₹2 lakh per year is awarded based on family income and academic performance.

Family income up to ₹3 lakh: 100% of the CGPA-based scholarship
₹3–6 lakh: 75% of the CGPA-based scholarship
₹6–9 lakh: 50% of the CGPA-based scholarship
Above ₹9 lakh: base scholarship only
From the second year onward, the additional scholarship depends on CGPA:

CGPA 7.5 or above: full additional ₹2 lakh
CGPA 6.6–7.4: proportional additional scholarship
CGPA 6.5 or below: no CGPA-based scholarship
To retain the scholarship each year, students must maintain at least 6.5 CGPA, pass all subjects, maintain 90% attendance in every course, follow the Student Code of Conduct, and submit updated family-income records. Serious conduct violations or failure to meet attendance requirements can affect the scholarship.

The program cost includes tuition, on-campus hostel accommodation, and meals (mess). A laptop is not included: every student must arrange one, with an expected cost of about ₹50,000. The other excluded cost is a one-time refundable ₹10,000 caution deposit payable to SRMU at admission.

The scholarship can continue through all four years, subject to annual review. In Year 4, students with a paid industry internship remain eligible for the CGPA-based scholarship. If a student does not secure a paid internship, the CGPA-based scholarship is not available for that year.

Explain internship lifecycle.
Sitare University
Sitare University's Twin

Our internship pathway is designed to build toward a full-time industry co-op in Year 4:

Years 1–2: preparation
Students build programming, problem-solving, and communication skills. COCO—our coding and communication summer program—runs after the first and second years to strengthen the skills employers look for.

After Year 2: early industry exposure
Most students undertake an industry internship after completing their second year, supported by our network of founders, venture capital partners, and companies. The fixed duration, compensation, and evaluation process for these internships are not specified.

Year 3: build substantial real-world capability
Students complete a 20-credit, two-semester project to architect and launch a large-scale system for real users. This work prepares them for the more intensive industry placement ahead.

Year 4: paid, year-long co-op
After Year 3, students move into a paid, year-long industry placement through campus recruitment. The co-op counts toward the B.Tech. degree, and students spend the year working in industry.

Placement and scholarship support
We actively support students with mentoring, preparation, and campus recruitment to secure the Year 4 placement. A student who secures a paid internship remains eligible for the CGPA-based scholarship. If a student does not secure one, the CGPA-based scholarship is not available for that year and the student must pay the full ₹2 lakh program cost.
